Location

Sultan Kudarat is nestled in the southwestern part of Mindanao island and belongs to the SOCCSKSARGEN region (Region XII). It shares borders with North Cotabato to the north, Maguindanao to the northwest, South Cotabato to the east, Sarangani to the southeast, and the Moro Gulf to the west.

The province covers a total land area of approximately 5,263 square kilometers. Its geography is incredibly diverse, stretching from towering mountain ranges in the interior to fertile river valleys and a long western coastline.

Overview

Sultan Kudarat was officially established as a province on June 22, 1973 through Presidential Decree No. 341. It was carved out from the old Cotabato province and proudly named after one of Mindanao's greatest heroes — Sultan Muhammad Dipatuan Kudarat, the fearless 17th-century Muslim ruler who fiercely resisted Spanish colonization.

Today, Sultan Kudarat is a thriving agricultural power. Its wide plains produce massive harvests of rice and corn, while its highland areas cultivate high-quality Arabica coffee and pineapples. Tacurong City serves as its vibrant urban hub for trade, education, and transportation.

Known Attractions

Must-visit landmarks and natural wonders

Provincial Capitol Building

Isulan

Standing proudly at the heart of Isulan, it features a massive golden dome that gleams brilliantly under the sun. Surrounded by beautifully landscaped gardens, it serves as a proud living symbol of Sultan Kudarat's identity and progress.

Baras Bird Sanctuary

Tacurong City

Home to tens of thousands of wild birds like herons and egrets. Every late afternoon, the sky transforms into a spectacular aerial show as massive flocks swirl and return to the riverside roosting trees.

La Palmera Mountain Ridge

Columbio

A breathtaking highland getaway with cool fresh air and panoramic views of rolling green hills and geometric pineapple plantations. Perfect for spectacular sunrises and camping enthusiasts.

Daguma Mountain Range

Eco-Adventure Frontier

Blanketed in ancient rainforests protecting rich wildlife and indigenous Teduray and Manobo ancestral domains. Offers challenging, raw trekking paths alongside stunning stream systems.

Underground Rivers & Caves

Subterranean Wonders

Carved over millions of years out of limestone rock, featuring towering stalactites, stalagmites, and crystal-clear streams. A thrilling spelunking environment for adventure seekers.

Moro Gulf Coastline

Western Coast

An undiscovered, pristine coastline boasting magnificent sunsets and marine-rich waters. It offers an unhurried, authentic local fishing vibe far removed from overcrowded commercial spots.

Culture & Traditions

Sultan Kudarat is a vibrant tri-people melting pot where Christian settlers, indigenous Maguindanaon Muslims, and Lumad tribes peacefully coexist.

  • Artistry: Intricate, bold geometric patterns found on Lumad handwoven fabrics, alongside the rhythmic beats of the Maguindanaon kulintang gongs.
  • Kalivungan Festival: Held every October, showcasing unity through vibrant street-dancing rivalries and broad agricultural exhibitions.
  • Kalimudan Festival: A beautiful tribute honoring the heritage, customs, and deep spiritual traditions of the Muslim communities.
